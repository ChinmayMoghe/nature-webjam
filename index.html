FIVE ELEMENTS

Earth

Earth represents solidness, The earth is rigid, heavy and stable.It can hold something and stop something.

Water

Water represents coldness. It helps to lubricate and possesses cohesive properties. Water is very flexible and can take any form when poured into any glass.Water represents adaptability and flexibility.

Fire

Fire signifies heat that makes things mature, assists in melting and purification. Fire can transform anything: it can transform solid to liquid, liquid to gas.

Wind

The wind signifies the unstable movement, cold and dry. The element must flow perfectly in life to have stability.

Space

Space defines the gap between two things, and it is also the element of non-resistance. Thus, it talks about how there should be some sort of space maintained between our joints, relationships, thoughts, and so on.
